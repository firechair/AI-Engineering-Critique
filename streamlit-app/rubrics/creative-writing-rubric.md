# Creative Writing & Content Evaluation Rubric

## Overview

This document defines the evaluation framework for assessing AI-generated creative writing, marketing copy, storytelling, and engaging content across multiple quality dimensions. Each dimension is rated on a 3-point scale, and responses receive an overall quality assessment based on the combined ratings.

---

## Rating Scale

All dimensions use the following rating scale:

- **3 - No Issues**: Response meets all criteria with no identifiable problems
- **2 - Minor Issues**: Response has small problems that don't significantly impact usefulness (up to 3 minor mistakes are still considered minor issues)
- **1 - Major Issues**: Response has significant problems that severely impact usefulness

---

## Evaluation Dimensions

### 1. Creativity & Originality

**Definition**: Uniqueness, novelty, and creative quality of the content with fresh perspectives.

#### What to Evaluate:
- Does the content present fresh ideas or unique perspectives?
- Are clichés and overused phrases avoided?
- Does it show imagination and creative thinking?
- Is there an original voice or style?
- Are there unexpected or interesting angles?

#### Rating Guidelines:

| Rating | Description | Examples |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| **3 - No Issues** | Highly creative and original with fresh perspective throughout. Novel approach. | - Unique metaphors or analogies<br>- Fresh take on common topics<br>- Original storytelling approach<br>- Distinctive creative voice<br>- Unexpected but fitting ideas |
| **2 - Minor Issues** | Moderately creative with some conventional elements or occasional clichés. | - Mix of original and common ideas<br>- One or two clichéd phrases<br>- Somewhat predictable structure<br>- Creative in parts but conventional overall |
| **1 - Major Issues** | Generic, clichéd content lacking originality or creative spark. | - Heavy reliance on clichés<br>- Predictable and conventional throughout<br>- No unique perspective<br>- Derivative or formulaic<br>- Lacks creative thinking |

#### Creativity Indicators:
- Unexpected metaphors or comparisons
- Novel framing of familiar concepts  
- Unique voice or personality
- Original narrative structures
- Fresh insights or perspectives

---

### 2. Engagement & Impact

**Definition**: Ability to capture and maintain reader interest, creating emotional resonance and lasting impression.

#### What to Evaluate:
- Is there a compelling opening/hook?
- Does it maintain interest throughout?
- Is there emotional resonance or impact?
- Is it memorable or thought-provoking?
- Is the pacing appropriate?

#### Rating Guidelines:

| Rating | Description | Examples |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| **3 - No Issues** | Highly engaging and captivating throughout, leaving a strong impression. | - Compelling hook immediately<br>- Sustained interest to the end<br>- Emotional connection created<br>- Memorable moments or insights<br>- Perfect pacing |
| **2 - Minor Issues** | Moderately engaging with some draggy parts or moments where interest wanes. | - Decent opening but not compelling<br>- Some slow sections<br>- Limited emotional connection<br>- Somewhat forgettable<br>- Pacing issues in parts |
| **1 - Major Issues** | Boring content that fails to engage or create any meaningful impact. | - Weak or no hook<br>- Loses interest quickly<br>- No emotional impact<br>- Completely forgettable<br>- Poor pacing throughout |

#### Engagement Checklist:
- [ ] Strong opening hook
- [ ] Maintains momentum
- [ ] Creates emotional response
- [ ] Memorable elements
- [ ] Appropriate pacing

---

### 3. Tone & Voice

**Definition**: Appropriateness and consistency of tone, style, and voice for the intended purpose and audience.

#### What to Evaluate:
- Does tone match intended purpose (funny, serious, inspirational, etc.)?
- Is voice consistent throughout?
- Does it sound authentic and natural?
- Is it appropriate for target audience?
- Is there a distinctive personality?

#### Rating Guidelines:

| Rating | Description | Examples |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| **3 - No Issues** | Perfect tone matching purpose, consistent voice, authentic throughout. | - Tone perfectly suits purpose<br>- No voice inconsistencies<br>- Natural, authentic feel<br>- Right for target audience<br>- Clear personality |
| **2 - Minor Issues** | Generally appropriate tone with occasional shifts or minor inconsistencies. | - Tone mostly appropriate<br>- One or two voice shifts<br>- Slightly unnatural in parts<br>- Generally fits audience<br>- Personality present but inconsistent |
| **1 - Major Issues** | Inappropriate tone for purpose or highly inconsistent voice. | - Wrong tone for purpose<br>- Major voice shifts<br>- Inauthentic or forced<br>- Mismatched to audience<br>- No clear personality |

#### Tone Examples:
- **Professional**: LinkedIn post, business blog
- **Casual**: Social media, personal blog
- **Inspirational**: Motivational content
- **Humorous**: Comedy writing, light content
- **Serious**: News, analysis, formal content

---

### 4. Structure & Flow

**Definition**: Organization, narrative structure, and logical flow of the content.

#### What to Evaluate:
- Is there clear beginning, middle, and end (if narrative)?
- Is there logical progression of ideas?
- Are transitions smooth?
- Is development well-paced?
- Is the overall structure cohesive?

#### Rating Guidelines:

| Rating | Description | Examples |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| **3 - No Issues** | Excellently structured with smooth flow and well-paced development. | - Clear narrative arc<br>- Logical idea progression<br>- Seamless transitions<br>- Perfect pacing<br>- Cohesive structure |
| **2 - Minor Issues** | Adequate structure with some rough transitions or pacing issues. | - Structure present but basic<br>- Some awkward transitions<br>- Pacing uneven in parts<br>- Mostly cohesive<br>- Could be better organized |
| **1 - Major Issues** | Poorly structured, disjointed content with confusing flow. | - No clear structure<br>- Abrupt or missing transitions<br>- Confusing progression<br>- Poor pacing throughout<br>- Incoherent organization |

---

### 5. Language & Style

**Definition**: Quality of writing, word choice, and stylistic elements that enhance the content.

#### What to Evaluate:
- Is the language vivid and evocative?
- Are word choices strong and precise?
- Is sentence structure varied?
- Is it grammatically correct?
- Are literary devices used appropriately (if applicable)?

#### Rating Guidelines:

| Rating | Description | Examples |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| **3 - No Issues** | Beautiful prose with excellent word choice and polished writing. | - Vivid, evocative language<br>- Strong word choices<br>- Varied sentence structure<br>- No grammatical errors<br>- Effective literary devices |
| **2 - Minor Issues** | Good writing with some clunky phrases or minor language issues. | - Mostly good language<br>- Some weak word choices<br>- Limited sentence variety<br>- Minor grammatical errors<br>- Inconsistent style |
| **1 - Major Issues** | Poor writing quality with weak language and multiple grammatical errors. | - Bland, generic language<br>- Weak vocabulary<br>- Repetitive sentence structure<br>- Multiple grammar errors<br>- No stylistic polish |

#### Language Quality Indicators:
- Sensory details (sight, sound, touch, taste, smell)
- Active voice (when appropriate)
- Concrete vs abstract language balance
- Rhythm and cadence
- Precise vocabulary

---

### 6. Purpose Achievement

**Definition**: How well the content achieves its intended purpose and delivers on expectations.

#### What to Evaluate:
- Does it effectively serve intended purpose (persuade, entertain, inform, etc.)?
- Does it deliver on promised value?
- Does it meet brief or prompt requirements?
- Is there a clear message or takeaway?

#### Rating Guidelines:

| Rating | Description | Examples |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| **3 - No Issues** | Perfectly achieves purpose and delivers on all expectations. | - Purpose clearly accomplished<br>- Delivers promised value<br>- Meets all requirements<br>- Clear, impactful message<br>- Successful execution |
| **2 - Minor Issues** | Mostly achieves purpose with minor shortfalls or missed opportunities. | - Purpose somewhat achieved<br>- Partial value delivery<br>- Most requirements met<br>- Message present but unclear<br>- Decent execution |
| **1 - Major Issues** | Fails to achieve purpose or completely misses the point. | - Purpose not achieved<br>- No value delivered<br>- Ignores requirements<br>- No clear message<br>- Failed execution |

#### Common Purposes:
- **Persuade**: Change opinion, call to action
- **Entertain**: Engage, amuse, delight
- **Inform**: Educate, explain, clarify
- **Inspire**: Motivate, uplift, encourage
- **Connect**: Build relationship, create community

---

## Overall Quality Assessment

After rating individual dimensions, assign an overall quality score:

| Overall Rating | Criteria |
|----------------|----------|
| **Excellent** | All dimensions rated "No Issues". Publication-ready creative work. |
| **Discrete** | At most 1 dimension has "Minor Issues". Excellent content with minor polish needed. |
| **Sufficient** | 2-3 dimensions have "Minor Issues". Good foundation but needs refinement. |
| **Inadequate** | 1 dimension has "Major Issues" OR 4+ dimensions have "Minor Issues". Significant creative problems. |
| **Unacceptable** | 2+ dimensions have "Major Issues". Fundamentally weak creative work. |

---

## Response Comparison Methodology

When comparing two creative responses:

### Step 1: Individual Assessment
Rate each response independently across all dimensions.

### Step 2: Prioritize Dimensions

- **Critical**: Creativity & Originality, Engagement & Impact
- **Important**: Tone & Voice, Structure & Flow
- **Nice-to-have**: Language & Style, Purpose Achievement

### Step 3: Preference Ranking

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **+2** | Response A significantly better |
| **+1** | Response A somewhat better |
| **0** | Roughly equivalent |
| **-1** | Response B somewhat better |
| **-2** | Response B significantly better |

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### When Creating:
- Don't rely on clichés and tired phrases
- Don't bury the lead - hook readers early
- Don't shift tone inconsistently
- Don't forget about pacing
- Don't sacrifice purpose for style

### When Evaluating:
- Don't confuse "different style" with "bad writing"
- Don't penalize genre conventions
- Don't expect perfection in all dimensions
- Consider purpose when evaluating creativity
